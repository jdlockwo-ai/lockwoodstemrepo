About LockwoodSTEM

Engineering, physics, and advanced manufacturing resources for aerospace-minded students.

LockwoodSTEM is a student-facing hub for course lessons, FabLab resources, engineering design workflows, and classroom systems used in the Academy of Advanced Aerospace Engineering at Cornerstone Charter Academy.

Engineering, physics, robotics, and FabLab learning built around hands-on problem solving and real documentation.

Mission

Make engineering feel real, useful, and possible.

This site is designed to help students move from ideas to evidence. Lessons connect design thinking, technical communication, CAD, programming, prototyping, testing, and reflection so students can build confidence as problem solvers.

Learning approach

Learn by building and documenting.

Students are expected to sketch, model, build, test, revise, and explain their decisions. The goal is not just completing a project; the goal is developing the habits engineers use to communicate and improve their work.

Course pathways

What students can find here

LockwoodSTEM organizes course materials, unit pages, lesson pages, resources, and FabLab supports in one place.

IED

Introduction to Engineering Design focuses on design process, sketching, measurement, CAD, prototyping, technical drawings, and portfolio-ready documentation.

POE

Principles of Engineering connects mechanisms, systems, structures, energy, circuits, data, and engineering analysis through applied design challenges.

ADM

Advanced Manufacturing introduces automation, robotic workcells, sensors, digital twins, cybersecurity, supply chain thinking, and Industry 4.0 workflows.
Academy of Advanced Aerospace Engineering

Aerospace as the design lens

The academy uses aerospace-themed problems to help students practice engineering habits in a focused context. Students apply classroom concepts to rockets, rovers, mechanisms, manufacturing systems, technical documentation, and design challenges.

  • Problem definition and criteria/constraints
  • Research-informed brainstorming and concept selection
  • CAD, prototyping, fabrication, and testing
  • Technical communication and presentation evidence
CCA FabLab

A classroom built for making

The FabLab supports hands-on learning with student workstations, digital fabrication tools, robotics equipment, engineering notebooks, and certification-based safety systems.

  • 3D printing and prototyping workflows
  • Laser cutting, shop safety, and tool certification
  • Robotics, automation, and programming practice
  • Documentation, inspection, and revision habits
Student habits

What this site reinforces

Document evidence

Notebook entries, sketches, screenshots, code, photos, test data, and reflections show how the work developed.

Revise with purpose

Students are encouraged to use feedback and testing results to make their designs stronger.

Work safely

FabLab access depends on safe habits, tool certification, PPE, and responsible use of equipment.

Communicate clearly

Strong engineering work includes clear decisions, constraints, technical details, and final explanations.
